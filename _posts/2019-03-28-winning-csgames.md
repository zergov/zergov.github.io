Hi, I'm Jonathan. I wanted to start blogging about stuff in 2019 but never really got into it. I think now is a good time.
I am currently studying software engineering at ETS (École de technologie supérieure), an engineering school in Montreal, Canada.

# What's the CS Games?
Every year, there is something called the [CS Games](http://csgames.org). CS Games stands for "Computer Science Games".
The CS Games are a collegiate competition that includes challenges from all aspects of computing. Many university from canada and from the US
meet up for a weekend and participate in different computer science challenges in the goal to win the CS Cup.

# CS Games 2018
Last year was my first participation at the CS Games. My university had two teams, team A and team B. Team A was made from
the cream of the cream, while team B was more of a team for the newer members. I was part of team B. Team A ended up winning the CS Games.

The whole CS Games was insane. I got hooked and was already excited for 2019.

# CS Games 2019
CS Game was all I had in mind in 2018-2019. I had to be in Team A, and I had to win my competitions / the CS Cup.

I managed to get into team A for ETS. __Boi__, was I happy.
All I had to do now was to prepare for my challenges, which were:
  - Web
  - Devops
  - Functional programming
  - Mobile developement

# Result
__We fucking won__. We won CS Games 2019, and on top of that, I got first place in all of my challenges.

Winning CS Games 2019 gave me the biggest high I have ever felt.
